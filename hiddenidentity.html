The Hidden Identity

THE HIDDEN IDENTITY

BY SENTIBILIN

Puts her makeup on while she plasters a smile,
On the red carpet she walks with her high heels on;
Her red dress conceals just for a while,
But her heart knows, when she gets home, it'll all be gone.

No one can see her hidden identity,
The secret life she lives when all the curtains fall;
Her husband to be blamed for her misery,
For all the tortur she receives behind the walls.

Fake identity defines her to the world,
But little do they know about the life she hides;
Insurmountable pain in her heart they crawl,
Her journal's the only friend her pain she confides.

Identity holds no meaning in her life,
When her real identity lies behind closed doors;
No matter all the hardships she has to strife,
Her own little world keeps crashing down the floor.
