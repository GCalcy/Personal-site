A Wandered Soul

A WANDERED SOUL

BY SENTIBILIN

A wandered soul in its own parade,
Soaring along the moonlight shade,
Gliding slow, through through the darksome lonely night;
A darkness ever so unveil'd,
And silence in its might prevail'd,
Awakening the fear that'll fore'er abide.

Quietly lay in dark estate,
As despair's what that's bound its fate,
With moans and sobs of grief in silent hours;
Spirit's low, the fear to quell,
And grievant heart that's bound to dwell,
For life's held captive behind the gravely bars.
